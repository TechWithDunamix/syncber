<FlashCardDeck>
  <FlashCard question="What does IAM stand for and what is its core purpose?" answer="Identity and Access Management — ensuring the right people have the right access to the right resources at the right time for the right reasons." />
  <FlashCard question="What are the four business drivers for IAM?" answer="1. Security — reducing breach risk. 2. Compliance — meeting regulatory mandates. 3. Operational efficiency — automating provisioning. 4. User experience — enabling SSO and self-service." />
  <FlashCard question="What are the three core capability areas of IAM?" answer="Identity Management (creating and storing identities), Access Management (authentication and authorization), and Governance (certification, SOD, auditing)." />
  <FlashCard question="True or False: IAM is a single software product." answer="False. IAM is a capability delivered through people, processes, and technology working together." />
  <FlashCard question="What are the four types of digital identities?" answer="1. User identity (human). 2. Service identity (API, microservice). 3. Device identity (laptop, server). 4. Workload identity (Kubernetes pod, cloud function)." />
  <FlashCard question="What is identity proofing?" answer="The process of collecting and verifying evidence about an identity before establishing a trusted digital identity." />
  <FlashCard question="What are the three NIST Identity Assurance Levels (IAL)?" answer="IAL1 — Self-attested, no proofing. IAL2 — Remote or in-person proofing (government ID, KBV). IAL3 — In-person physical proofing with biometric comparison." />
  <FlashCard question="What is identity federation?" answer="Federation enables an identity created in one domain to be trusted in another — the foundation of cross-organisational SSO." />
  <FlashCard question="What does JML stand for in identity lifecycle management?" answer="Joiner-Mover-Leaver — the framework for managing identities from creation through modification to removal." />
  <FlashCard question="What three actions are required immediately when a user leaves an organisation?" answer="1. Account disable (prevent login). 2. Credential revocation (reset password, invalidate sessions). 3. Access removal (remove group memberships, roles)." />
  <FlashCard question="What is the most common security finding related to identity lifecycle?" answer="Delayed deprovisioning — accounts remaining active after a user has left the organisation." />
  <FlashCard question="What is the foundational integration needed for lifecycle automation?" answer="HR system (HRIS) integration — connecting IAM to the authoritative source for identity events (new hires, terminations, transfers)." />
  <FlashCard question="What is the primary difference between a directory service and a relational database?" answer="Directory services are hierarchical (tree structure), read-optimised, and use LDAP. Relational databases are tabular, transaction-oriented, and use SQL." />
  <FlashCard question="What protocol does LDAP stand for and what does it define?" answer="Lightweight Directory Access Protocol — defines the data model (DIT), query syntax (LDAP filters), operations (Bind, Search, Modify), and security (SASL, LDAPS)." />
  <FlashCard question="Name three cloud directory services." answer="Azure AD (Entra ID), Okta Universal Directory, Google Cloud Identity, Amazon Cognito." />
  <FlashCard question="What is the hub-and-spoke directory integration pattern?" answer="A central identity directory serves as the authentication source for multiple target applications, rather than each application maintaining its own identity store." />
  <FlashCard question="What is Just-in-Time (JIT) provisioning?" answer="Accounts are created on first successful authentication — no pre-provisioning needed. Ideal for SaaS applications where licensing depends on active users." />
  <FlashCard question="What does SCIM stand for?" answer="System for Cross-domain Identity Management (RFC 7642–7644) — the open RESTful standard for automating identity provisioning." />
  <FlashCard question="What HTTP methods does SCIM use for provisioning operations?" answer="POST — Create user. GET — Read user. PATCH — Partial update. PUT — Full replace. DELETE — Remove user." />
  <FlashCard question="What is the difference between account disable and account deletion in deprovisioning?" answer="Disable prevents login but preserves data (reversible). Deletion permanently removes the account (irreversible). Disable should happen immediately; deletion after the retention period." />
  <FlashCard question="What are the three main authentication factor types?" answer="Knowledge (something you know), Possession (something you have), Inherence (something you are). True MFA requires factors from at least two different categories." />
  <FlashCard question="What percentage of automated attacks does MFA block according to Microsoft?" answer="99.9% — MFA is the single most effective security control organisations can deploy." />
  <FlashCard question="What is MFA fatigue?" answer="An attack where the adversary repeatedly sends push notification MFA requests until the user eventually accepts one, granting the attacker access." />
  <FlashCard question="What makes FIDO2/WebAuthn phishing-resistant?" answer="FIDO2 uses public-key cryptography where the private key never leaves the user's device. The key is bound to the origin domain, so phishing sites cannot use stolen credentials." />
  <FlashCard question="What is the difference between authentication and authorization?" answer="Authentication verifies identity (&quot;Who are you?&quot;). Authorization determines permissions (&quot;What are you allowed to do?&quot;)." />
  <FlashCard question="What are the three core RBAC rules according to NIST?" answer="1. Role assignment — user must be assigned a role. 2. Role authorization — user's active role must be authorised. 3. Permission authorization — permission must be authorised for the active role." />
  <FlashCard question="What attributes does ABAC use to make access decisions?" answer="User attributes, resource attributes, action attributes, and environmental context (time, network, location)." />
  <FlashCard question="What is ReBAC and what system popularised it?" answer="Relationship-Based Access Control — models access as relationships between entities in a graph. Popularised by Google Zanzibar (used in Google Drive, YouTube, Gmail)." />
  <FlashCard question="What is access certification?" answer="Periodic review of user entitlements by resource owners and managers to confirm or revoke each user's access." />
  <FlashCard question="What is segregation of duties (SoD)?" answer="Ensuring no single person has enough access to commit fraud or cause significant damage. Conflicting access combinations must be identified and prevented." />
  <FlashCard question="What compliance frameworks require IAM controls?" answer="Multiple — SOX, GDPR, HIPAA, PCI DSS, ISO 27001, NIST SP 800-53 all mandate identity and access management controls." />
  <FlashCard question="What are the five levels of the IAM maturity model?" answer="1. Initial (ad-hoc). 2. Defined (documented). 3. Managed (automated). 4. Measured (continuous). 5. Optimized (predictive)." />
  <FlashCard question="What is the most common IAM architecture pattern in enterprises undergoing cloud migration?" answer="Hybrid IAM — combining on-premises directories (AD) with cloud identity platforms (Azure AD, Okta) through synchronisation." />
  <FlashCard question="What are the three principles of Zero Trust?" answer="1. Verify explicitly — authenticate and authorize every request. 2. Least privilege — grant minimum access required. 3. Assume breach — segment, monitor, trust nothing by default." />
  <FlashCard question="What are the components of a policy-based authorization system?" answer="PEP (Policy Enforcement Point) — intercepts requests. PDP (Policy Decision Point) — evaluates policies. PIP (Policy Information Point) — provides attribute data." />
  <FlashCard question="When should an organisation choose centralized vs. decentralized IAM?" answer="Centralized for small-medium, single-jurisdiction, organic-growth organisations. Decentralized for large, multi-region enterprises with acquisition history and diverse regulatory requirements." />
