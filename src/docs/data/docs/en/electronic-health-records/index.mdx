Welcome to **Electronic Health Records Management** — your complete guide to understanding, implementing, and managing electronic health record systems in modern healthcare.

Major transformations in health information technology (IT) have given us access to various data and services at our fingertips. With health IT, providers can now reach out to patients, hospitals, pharmacies, laboratories, and other organizations that make up the circle of care. At the heart of this transformation is the **Electronic Health Record (EHR)** — a digital version of paper medical charts that has fundamentally changed how healthcare is documented, shared, and improved.

<Aside variant="info" title="Course Format">
  Self-paced · 4 phases · 32 modules · 34 course pages · Real-world case studies, practical examples, and hands-on exercises included.
</Aside>

## What You'll Learn

<Steps>
1. **Phase 1: EHR Foundations** (8 modules) — What is an EHR, EHR vs EMR, contents, documentation importance, history, data standards, regulations, patient engagement
2. **Phase 2: Transition & Processes** (8 modules) — Paper-based procedures, transition to EHR, contributors/ownership, workflow redesign, implementation process, training/change management, data migration, quality measures
3. **Phase 3: EHR Software & Systems** (8 modules) — EHR software capabilities, CDS, PMS, interoperability, e-prescribing, telehealth, population health tools, coding/billing, analytics/reporting
4. **Phase 4: Impact & Outcomes** (8 modules) — Advantages, disaster response, patient safety, provider burnout, future trends, cost-benefit analysis, data analytics, ROI
</Steps>
