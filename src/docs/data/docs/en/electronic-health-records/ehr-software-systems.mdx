The basic function of an EHR software is to standardize the functionality of being interconnected with other systems so that health information may be shared with different providers, hospitals, and health institutions. Modern EHR platforms are sophisticated systems that integrate clinical, administrative, and financial workflows.

## Commercial EHR System Capabilities

Commercial electronic health record systems offer a comprehensive set of capabilities:

| Capability | Description | Primary User |
|------------|-------------|--------------|
| **Patient Portal** | Secure online access for patients to view records, communicate with providers, request appointments | Patients |
| **Storage of Office Forms** | Digital storage and retrieval of completed forms | Administrative staff |
| **Progress Notes** | Electronic documentation using templates or free text | Providers |
| **CPOE Templates** | Structured order entry for medications, labs, and referrals | Providers |
| **Report and Image Functionalities** | Viewing and reporting on radiology images and lab results | Providers |
| **Data Capture for Vital Signs** | Direct entry and graphing of patient vital signs | Clinical staff |
| **Email and Intraoffice Messaging** | Secure communication between staff members | All staff |
| **Fax Functions** | Electronic fax transmission and receipt | Administrative staff |
| **Documentation Templates** | User-defined, pre-defined templates or free text documentation | Providers |
| **Patient Reminders** | Automated appointment and preventive care reminders | Automated / Admin |
| **Auto-Notification of Results** | Automatic alerts when lab results or data are available | Providers |
| **Print and Summary Functions** | Generation of visit summaries, referral letters, and forms | All staff |
| **E-Signature Insertion** | Digital signature for orders, notes, and prescriptions | Providers |
| **Review of Incoming Lab Results** | Secure receipt and review of laboratory data | Providers |
| **Importation of Lab Data** | Electronic import from reference laboratories | Automated |

### Patient Portal Features

The patient portal is a critical component of modern EHR systems that empowers patients to participate in their own healthcare:

```yaml
Patient Portal Capabilities:
  └─ View and download health records
  └─ Request appointments and view upcoming visits
  └─ Send secure messages to providers
  └─ View lab and test results
  └─ Request prescription refills
  └─ Complete pre-visit forms electronically
  └─ View immunization records
  └─ Make payments online
  └─ Access educational materials
  └─ Update demographics and contact information

Patient Portal Benefits:
  └─ Improved patient satisfaction (85% of patients rate portals positively)
  └─ Reduced phone call volume to practices (30-40% reduction)
  └─ Increased preventive care adherence (15-25% improvement)
  └─ Better medication adherence via refill requests
  └─ Fewer missed appointments with automated reminders
```

### Progress Notes Templates

EHR systems support multiple formats for clinical documentation:

| Template Type | Description | Best For |
|--------------|-------------|----------|
| **Pre-Defined Templates** | Built-in templates based on specialty (e.g., cardiology, pediatrics) | Standardized visits |
| **User-Defined Templates** | Custom templates created by providers or organizations | Practice-specific needs |
| **Free Text** | Unstructured narrative documentation | Complex or unique encounters |
| **Structured Data Entry** | Drop-downs, checkboxes, and coded fields | Research and analytics |
| **Voice Recognition** | Dictation-to-text documentation | High-volume providers |

## Clinical Decision Support (CDS)

**Clinical decision support (CDS)** helps providers modify patient care needs. It makes sure that providers follow issued procedures on patients' diagnoses and allows them to make evidence-based decisions at the point of care.

### CDS Functions

```yaml
CDS Capabilities:
  Database Scanning:
    └─ Identify patients meeting specific criteria
    └─ Example: Search for all diabetic patients over 65
    └─ Example: Identify patients due for mammogram
    └─ Enables population health management

  Patient Summaries and Reports:
    └─ Generate comprehensive patient summaries
    └─ Create condition-specific reports
    └─ Produce handoff reports for care transitions
    └─ Generate patient education materials

  Diagnosis-Specific Templates:
    └─ Documentation outlines tailored to specific diagnoses
    └─ Example: CHF follow-up template with weight tracking
    └─ Example: Diabetes management template with HbA1c trends
    └─ Ensures consistent, complete documentation

  Screening Recommendations:
    └─ Automated reminders for preventive screenings
    └─ Based on patient age, gender, and risk factors
    └─ Example: "Patient due for colonoscopy — age 50"
    └─ Example: "HbA1c overdue — last test 14 months ago"

  Evidence-Based Treatment Planning:
    └─ Treatment recommendations based on clinical guidelines
    └─ Example: Heart failure treatment following ACC/AHA guidelines
    └─ Example: Hepatitis C treatment protocols
    └─ Reduces variation in care
```

### Types of CDS Interventions

| CDS Type | Example | Alert Level |
|----------|---------|-------------|
| **Drug Allergy Alerts** | "Patient has allergy to penicillin — amoxicillin ordered" | Hard stop or override |
| **Drug-Drug Interactions** | "Warfarin and aspirin increase bleeding risk" | Warning with override |
| **Duplicate Therapy** | "Patient already on lisinopril — another ACE inhibitor ordered" | Warning |
| **Dosing Support** | "Creatinine clearance 30 — reduce metformin dose" | Advisory |
| **Preventive Care Reminders** | "Mammogram due — patient is 52 years old" | Best practice alert |
| **Guideline Adherence** | "HbA1c > 9% — consider medication adjustment" | Best practice alert |
| **Diagnosis Support** | "Symptoms suggest possible DVT — consider ultrasound" | Advisory |

### How CDS Improves Healthcare

```yaml
Patient Safety Improvements:
  └─ Reduces medication errors by preventing wrong drug, dose, or route
  └─ Alerts to potential allergic reactions
  └─ Identifies dangerous drug interactions before prescribing
  └─ Ensures appropriate monitoring for high-risk medications

Quality of Care Improvements:
  └─ Increases adherence to evidence-based guidelines
  └─ Reduces unnecessary variation in clinical practice
  └─ Promotes appropriate use of diagnostic tests
  └─ Ensures timely preventive care and screenings

Cost Reductions:
  └─ Reduces unnecessary testing and duplicate orders
  └─ Prevents adverse drug events that require hospitalization
  └─ Shortens length of stay through improved care coordination
  └─ Reduces malpractice risk through documented clinical reasoning
```

### CDS Effectiveness Factors

The CDS tools work effectively when information has been entered properly into the system:

```yaml
Factors for Effective CDS:
  └─ Data Quality: Complete, accurate, and structured data entry
  └─ Template Adherence: Following correct format and guidelines
  └─ Timely Alerts: Presented at the right point in the workflow
  └─ Actionable Information: Clear recommendations, not just warnings
  └─ Clinical Relevance: Alerts based on evidence, not administrative rules
  └─ Minimal Alert Fatigue: High-value alerts only — avoid excessive warnings

Common CDS Challenges:
  └─ Alert fatigue: Too many alerts lead to clinicians ignoring warnings
  └─ Workflow disruption: Alerts interrupt clinical workflow
  └─ Data entry errors: Incorrect data leads to incorrect recommendations
  └─ Override rates: Up to 90% of CDS alerts are overridden
  └─ Maintenance burden: Clinical knowledge must be continuously updated
```

<Aside variant="info" title="CDS and Provider Decision-Making">
  With proper data entry and template adherence, providers can make critical decisions based on available information — including patient needs, examination data, and treatment protocols. CDS improves patient safety, reduces costs from unnecessary testing, and improves clinical outcomes. Providers can also share best practices on diagnosis and treatment using CDS-enabled systems.
</Aside>

## EHR Interoperability

Interoperability — the ability of different EHR systems to exchange and use health information — is critical for coordinated care:

| Interoperability Level | Description | Standard |
|-----------------------|-------------|----------|
| **Foundational** | Systems can send and receive data | Basic data transport |
| **Structural** | Systems can interpret data at the data field level | HL7 v2, CCD/CDA |
| **Semantic** | Systems can understand the meaning of the data | SNOMED, LOINC, RxNorm |
| **Organizational** | Governance, policy, and workflow integration | Care coordination agreements |

**Modern Interoperability Standards:**

```yaml
HL7 FHIR (Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources):
  └─ Modern RESTful API-based standard
  └─ Supports mobile and web applications
  └─ Required by 21st Century Cures Act
  └─ Enables patient access via smartphone apps

HL7 v2:
  └─ Most widely implemented healthcare messaging standard
  └─ Supports ADT (Admission, Discharge, Transfer), orders, results
  └─ Still the backbone of hospital integration

CCD (Continuity of Care Document):
  └─ XML-based document for patient summary exchange
  └─ Used for transitions of care
  └─ Contains allergies, medications, problems, procedures
```

## Key Takeaways

- Commercial EHR systems offer comprehensive capabilities including patient portals, CPOE, e-prescribing, progress notes templates, lab integration, and e-signature
- Patient portals empower patients with access to their health information, secure messaging, appointment requests, and prescription refills
- Clinical Decision Support (CDS) helps providers follow evidence-based guidelines through alerts, reminders, and structured templates
- CDS alerts include drug-allergy warnings, drug-drug interactions, dosing support, preventive care reminders, and guideline adherence prompts
- For CDS to work effectively, data must be entered properly following correct format and template guidelines
- CDS improves patient safety, reduces costs, improves clinical outcomes, and enables providers to share best practices
- Interoperability standards (HL7 FHIR, HL7 v2, CCD) enable different EHR systems to exchange health information
- Alert fatigue (up to 90% override rate) is the most significant challenge to CDS effectiveness
