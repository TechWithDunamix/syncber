<FlashCardDeck>
  <FlashCard question="What is an Electronic Health Record (EHR)?" answer="A digital or electronic version of a patient's medical history or chart. It contains real-time, secure, and instantly accessible medical information for authorized users. It supports healthcare activities and strengthens the relationship between patients and clinicians." />
  
  <FlashCard question="What is the difference between an EHR and an EMR?" answer="An EMR (Electronic Medical Record) is limited to medical/clinical data from a single practice. An EHR goes beyond clinical data and is designed to share information across all healthcare providers involved in a patient's care, including laboratories, specialists, and hospitals." />
  
  <FlashCard question="Who owns the patient's medical record?" answer="The facility or individuals who created the record own it. For example, data created in a hospital belongs to that institution. However, patients have the legal right to access their medical information anytime through a medical release form." />
  
  <FlashCard question="What are the three classifications of EHR contents?" answer="1. Administrative Information (billing, demographics, insurance). 2. Clinical Information (medical history, diagnoses, vital signs, medications, test results). 3. Legal Forms (consent forms, release of information, advance directives)." />
  
  <FlashCard question="What does SOAP charting stand for?" answer="S — Subjective (what the patient says). O — Objective (what the provider observes/measures). A — Assessment (diagnosis based on S and O). P — Plan (treatment, medications, follow-up)." />
  
  <FlashCard question="What is the CMS-1500 form used for?" answer="It is the standard claim form used to submit healthcare claims to insurance companies for reimbursement. It contains patient information, diagnosis codes (ICD-10), procedure codes (CPT), and provider information." />
  
  <FlashCard question="What is the Meaningful Use (MU) program?" answer="A payment incentive program for physicians and eligible specialists who use and implement EHR technology following specified requirements. It had three stages: data capture (Stage 1), advanced clinical processes (Stage 2), and improved outcomes (Stage 3). It was later renamed the Promoting Interoperability Program." />
  
  <FlashCard question="What does CPOE stand for and what does it do?" answer="Computerized Provider Order Entry. It allows providers to enter and send treatment orders or instructions electronically — including medications, lab tests, imaging, and referrals. CPOE has been shown to reduce medication errors by 48-81%." />
  
  <FlashCard question="What is Clinical Decision Support (CDS)?" answer="CDS helps providers modify patient care needs by ensuring they follow evidence-based procedures. It includes alerts (drug interactions, allergies), screening reminders, diagnosis-specific templates, and evidence-based treatment guidelines." />
  
  <FlashCard question="What is the difference between PMS and EHR software?" answer="Practice Management Software (PMS) handles administrative and financial operations — scheduling, demographics, billing, claims. EHR software focuses on clinical documentation, order entry, e-prescribing, and clinical decision support. Many modern platforms integrate both." />
  
  <FlashCard question="What is the doctrine of professional discretion?" answer="It allows the provider to use their best judgment in sharing clinical observations with patients who have unstable conditions (such as those being treated for mental or emotional disturbances). It is meant to protect patients from harm when health information is shared." />
  
  <FlashCard question="What is a superbill in medical billing?" answer="A document that indicates the total charges incurred based on services provided to a patient. It includes diagnosis codes (ICD-10), procedure codes (CPT), charges, payments, and patient information. The superbill is used as the source document for completing the CMS-1500 claim form." />
  
  <FlashCard question="What are the three essential health IT features for disaster response?" answer="1. On-Site Safety — physical protection of local systems. 2. Off-Site Data — geographically separate backup. 3. Accessibility — web-based access from any location during emergencies." />
  
  <FlashCard question="What patient rights does HIPAA guarantee?" answer="Right to Access records, Right to Amend incorrect information, Right to Request Restrictions on use/disclosure, Right to Accounting of Disclosures, Right to Request Confidential Communications, and Right to File a Complaint." />
  
  <FlashCard question="What is the HITECH Act?" answer="The Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health Act, part of the 2009 Recovery Act. It provided $27 billion in financial incentives for EHR adoption, created the Meaningful Use program, established EHR certification criteria, expanded HIPAA requirements, and funded health IT training." />
  
  <FlashCard question="How much did EHR adoption increase from 2007 to 2024?" answer="From approximately 17% adoption in 2007-2008 to approximately 96% in 2024 — driven by federal financial incentives and later payment penalties for non-adopters." />
  
  <FlashCard question="What is the principle of documentation in healthcare?" answer=""If it was not documented, then it never happened." This principle has legal, financial, and clinical implications and drives all healthcare documentation requirements." />
  
  <FlashCard question="What is a patient portal?" answer="A secure online application that gives patients access to their health records, lab results, appointment scheduling, prescription refills, secure messaging with providers, and educational materials. Patient portals increase engagement and satisfaction." />
  
  <FlashCard question="What is the purpose of the account ledger in PMS?" answer="To track the patient's financial transactions including charges, payments (patient and insurance), adjustments (contractual write-offs), and the current balance owed." />
  
  <FlashCard question="What are the four phases of the NIST incident response lifecycle?" answer="Wait — that is from cybersecurity, not EHR! EHR-relevant frameworks include the three stages of Meaningful Use and the EHR implementation lifecycle: Planning, Selection, Implementation, Go-Live, Optimization." />
  
  <FlashCard question="What is the difference between a hard stop and an advisory CDS alert?" answer="A hard stop prevents the provider from proceeding (e.g., ordering a medication the patient is allergic to) until the alert is addressed. An advisory provides information and recommendations but allows the provider to override and proceed." />
  
  <FlashCard question="How does an EHR improve care coordination?" answer="By integrating patient health information and distributing it to all authorized providers involved in care. This is especially important for patients seeing multiple specialists, receiving emergency treatment, or transitioning between care settings." />
  
  <FlashCard question="What is the day sheet in medical practice accounting?" answer="A document that contains checks and balances for daily bank deposits. It tracks all daily transactions including charges, payments, adjustments, and new balance totals — ensuring financial reconciliation." />
  
  <FlashCard question="What is alert fatigue in healthcare IT?" answer="When clinicians receive so many CDS alerts (drug interactions, allergy warnings, reminders) that they become desensitized and begin overriding alerts without reading them. Up to 90% of CDS alerts are overridden, reducing the effectiveness of clinical decision support." />
  
  <FlashCard question="What is the Promoting Interoperability Program?" answer="The successor to the Meaningful Use program (renamed in 2018). It focuses on electronic prescribing, health information exchange, patient access to records via API, and public health reporting. Non-adopters face Medicare payment penalties." />
