Patient safety is the most compelling argument for EHR adoption. The landmark 1999 Institute of Medicine report "To Err Is Human" estimated that 44,000-98,000 Americans die annually from preventable medical errors — making it a leading cause of death. EHRs have proven to be one of the most effective tools for reducing these errors.

## The Problem of Medical Errors

```yaml
Types of Medical Errors:
  └− Medication Errors (most common):
       Wrong drug: Similar name, misread prescription (25%)
       Wrong dose: Decimal error, calculation error (20%)
       Wrong route: Oral given IV, etc. (10%)
       Wrong patient: Similar names, misidentified patient (15%)
       Drug interaction: Previously unknown interaction (15%)
       Allergy: Known allergy not checked (15%)

  └− Diagnostic Errors:
       Missed diagnosis: Symptoms not recognized
       Delayed diagnosis: Test results not followed up
       Wrong diagnosis: Misinterpretation of findings

  └− Communication Errors:
       Handoff failures: Information lost during shift change
       Referral failures: Referral made but not completed
       Discharge failures: Instructions not communicated

  └− Procedural Errors:
       Wrong-site surgery
       Retained foreign objects
       Equipment misuse
```

## How EHRs Reduce Errors

### Medication Error Reduction

| Error Type | Paper System | EHR Prevention | Impact |
|------------|-------------|----------------|--------|
| **Illegible handwriting** | 15% of handwritten prescriptions have errors | Typed/selected prescriptions always legible | Eliminates legibility errors |
| **Wrong drug selection** | Drug names look/sound alike | Drop-down selection, no free-text drug entry | 80% reduction in look-alike errors |
| **Wrong dose** | Decimal errors (e.g., .5mg vs 0.5mg) | Dose range checking, decimal forcing functions | 90% reduction in decimal errors |
| **Drug interaction** | Provider must recall all interactions | Real-time interaction checking at time of prescribing | 65% reduction in preventable ADEs |
| **Allergy check** | Provider must recall known allergies | Hard-stop alert for known allergies | Near-elimination of allergy-related errors |
| **Wrong patient** | Paper chart may be wrong chart | Patient identification step before ordering | 95% reduction in wrong-patient orders |

### CPOE Impact

Computerized Provider Order Entry (CPOE) with clinical decision support is the single most effective EHR intervention for patient safety:

```yaml
CPOE Safety Benefits:
  └− Medication errors: 48-81% reduction (The Leapfrog Group)
  └− Adverse drug events: 30-50% reduction
  └− Serious medication errors: 55% reduction
  └− Transfusion errors: 50% reduction
  └− Lab test ordering errors: 30% reduction

Key CPOE Safety Features:
  └− Structured order sets (condition-specific, evidence-based)
  └− Default dose, frequency, and duration
  └− Maximum dose alerts
  └− Duplicate order checking
  └− Corollary orders (e.g., ordering gentamicin → prompts for renal function monitoring)
  └− Order suspensions (e.g., holding anticoagulation before procedure)
```

### Barcode Medication Administration (BCMA)

BCMA uses barcodes to ensure the "five rights" of medication administration:

```yaml
The Five Rights + BCMA:
  1. Right Patient: Scan patient wristband barcode
  2. Right Medication: Scan medication barcode
  3. Right Dose: System verifies dose matches order
  4. Right Route: System verifies route matches order
  5. Right Time: System verifies timing matches order
  
  BCMA also checks:
     └− Is the medication due NOW?
     └− Has the medication already been given?
     └− Are there any new allergies?
     └− Are there any new interactions?

BCMA Impact:
  └− 77% reduction in medication administration errors
  └− 44% reduction in transcription errors
  └− Near-elimination of wrong-patient medication errors
  └− Improved documentation accuracy for administered medications

Common BCMA Workflow:
  1. Nurse logs into medication administration record (MAR)
  2. Selects patient from assignment list
  3. Scans patient wristband barcode
  4. System displays due medications
  5. Nurse scans each medication barcode
  6. System verifies: right patient, drug, dose, route, time
  7. If mismatch → alert — medication not administered
  8. If match → medication administered, MAR updated
```

## Clinical Decision Support for Safety

| CDS Type | Safety Purpose | Alert Level |
|----------|---------------|-------------|
| **Drug-Allergy Check** | Prevent allergic reactions | Hard stop |
| **Drug-Drug Interaction** | Prevent adverse interactions | Warning with override option |
| **Drug-Disease Contraindication** | Prevent harm from pre-existing conditions | Warning |
| **Duplicate Therapy Check** | Prevent medication duplication | Warning |
| **Dose Range Checking** | Prevent overdose or underdose | Hard stop for maximum dose |
| **Guideline Adherence** | Ensure evidence-based care | Best practice advisory |
| **Preventive Care Alert** | Ensure timely screening | Reminder |

<Aside variant="danger" title="The Paradox of Safety Alerts">
  While CDS alerts improve safety, excessive alerts cause "alert fatigue" — clinicians override up to 90% of alerts without reading them. Organizations must carefully tune alerts to preserve the safety benefit while minimizing desensitization. Each alert should present a meaningful risk that requires human judgment.
</Aside>

## Diagnostic Safety

EHRs help reduce diagnostic errors through:

```yaml
Diagnostic Safety Features:
  └− Clinical History Availability:
       Complete history available at every encounter
       Provider sees: past diagnoses, test results, consultant notes
       Reduces: missed connections between current symptoms and past findings
  
  └− Results Tracking:
       Test results tracked to completion
       Abnormal results flagged for provider review
       Critical results trigger immediate alert
       Never "lost" lab results
  
  └− Follow-Up Management:
       Automated follow-up reminders
       Referral tracking — closed-loop confirmation
       Missed appointment notification
  
  └− Decision Support for Diagnosis:
       Differential diagnosis prompts
       Symptom-based diagnostic aids
       Evidence-based testing recommendations
```

## Safety Event Reporting

EHRs enable systematic reporting and analysis of safety events:

| Event Type | Description | EHR Data Source |
|------------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Medication Error** | Wrong drug, dose, route, patient | CPOE logs, MAR, pharmacy system |
| **Near Miss** | Error caught before reaching patient | CDS alert logs, pharmacy intervention |
| **Adverse Drug Event** | Patient harmed by medication | Clinical documentation, incident reports |
| **Diagnostic Delay** | Delayed diagnosis | Test completion tracking, referral tracking |
| **Wrong-Site Surgery** | Procedure on wrong body part | Surgical safety checklist in EHR |
| **Patient Fall** | Patient falls during care | Nursing documentation, incident reports |

## Safety Metrics

| Metric | Target | Data Source |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| **Medication Error Rate** | < 1 per 1,000 doses | EHR medication administration records |
| **ADE Rate** | < 2 per 1,000 patient days | Clinical documentation, trigger tools |

## Key Takeaways

- The IOM's "To Err Is Human" (1999) found 44,000-98,000 annual deaths from preventable errors — EHRs are designed to address the root causes
- CPOE with CDS reduces medication errors by 48-81% through legible orders, dose checking, drug interaction alerts, allergy alerts, and wrong-patient safeguards
- Barcode Medication Administration (BCMA) ensures the five rights (patient, drug, dose, route, time) and reduces administration errors by 77%
- CDS alerts for safety include drug-allergy (hard stop), drug-drug interactions (warning), dose range checking (hard stop for max dose), and duplicate therapy (warning)
- Alert fatigue is a significant problem — up to 90% of alerts are overridden, requiring careful tuning to preserve safety benefits
- Diagnostic safety improves through complete clinical history availability, results tracking, automated follow-up, and diagnostic decision support
- Safety event reporting systems track medication errors, near misses, ADEs, diagnostic delays, and other adverse events for systematic improvement
- Key safety metrics: medication error rate, CPOE utilization, BCMA compliance, alert override rate, and critical result notification time
- The safest EHR is one that balances protection (alerts, checks) with usability (minimizing false alarms, fitting workflow)
- EHRs are not inherently safe — they require proper implementation, tuning, and human oversight to realize their safety potential
