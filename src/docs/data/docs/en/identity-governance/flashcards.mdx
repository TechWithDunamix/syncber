<FlashCardDeck>
  <FlashCard question="What is Identity Governance?" answer="The discipline of ensuring that the right people have the right access to the right resources — and that this access is properly controlled, reviewed, and auditable. It provides the accountability layer of IAM." />
  <FlashCard question="What are the four phases of the identity lifecycle?" answer="Joiner (account creation, role assignment), Mover (role changes, transfers, promotions), Leaver (account deactivation, data handover), and Rehire (account restoration, entitlement review)." />
  <FlashCard question="What is access certification?" answer="Periodic reviews where managers and resource owners validate whether users still need their current access. Certification campaigns are the primary mechanism for detecting and remediating privilege creep." />
  <FlashCard question="What is Segregation of Duties (SoD)?" answer="Policies that prevent any single individual from holding conflicting permissions that could enable fraud or abuse. SoD analysis detects toxic permission combinations across all of a user's roles and entitlements." />
  <FlashCard question="What is role mining?" answer="The process of discovering natural role groupings from existing access data. Role mining analyses current user entitlements to identify patterns that can be used to design a role-based access control model." />
  <FlashCard question="What are the consequences of inadequate identity governance?" answer="SOX violations, GDPR fines, insider fraud, data breaches, and the inability to answer the most basic audit question: &quot;Who has access to what, and is that access appropriate?&quot;" />
  <FlashCard question="What is identity analytics?" answer="Advanced analytics that detect anomalies in identity behaviour — dormant accounts, unusual access patterns, privilege escalation, and potential insider threats. Analytics transform IGA from reactive compliance into proactive security." />
  <FlashCard question="What is the relationship between IGA and GRC?" answer="IGA is the governance pillar of GRC (Governance, Risk, and Compliance). It provides the data and controls that feed risk management (which access combinations create risk) and compliance (proving access is appropriately controlled)." />
  <FlashCard question="What are the key capabilities of an IGA platform?" answer="Identity lifecycle management, access certification campaigns, segregation of duties analysis, role management and mining, identity analytics, self-service access requests, compliance reporting, and policy enforcement." />
  <FlashCard question="What compliance frameworks require identity governance controls?" answer="SOX (Section 404 — internal controls), GDPR (Article 5 — accountability), HIPAA (access control for ePHI), PCI DSS v4.0 (Requirement 7/9 — need-to-know and access review), ISO 27001 (A.9.2 — user access provisioning and review), and NIST SP 800-53 (AC-2 — account management)." />
  <FlashCard question="What is self-service IAM in the context of governance?" answer="Access requests, approval workflows, password management, and user experience features that allow users to request and manage their own access while maintaining governance controls through automated approval chains." />
  <FlashCard question="What is privilege creep and how is it detected?" answer="Privilege creep is the gradual accumulation of excessive permissions over time as users change roles. It is detected through regular access certification campaigns and identity analytics that flag users with permissions exceeding their current role requirements." />
</FlashCardDeck>
